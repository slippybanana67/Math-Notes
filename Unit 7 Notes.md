# Unit 7 – Quadrilaterals and Polygons

---

## 7.1 – Angles of Polygons

### Polygon
A closed shape formed by joining line segments.

### Diagonal of a Polygon
A line segment that connects two **non-consecutive** vertices of a polygon.

### Convex vs. Concave Polygons

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Convex** | A polygon where all diagonals lie within the shape |
| **Concave** | A polygon where at least one diagonal lies outside the shape |

### Polygon Interior Angles Theorem
The sum of the interior angles of a convex **n-gon** is:
> Sum = (n − 2) · 180°

| Polygon | Sides (n) | Interior Angle Sum |
|---|---|---|
| Triangle | 3 | 180° |
| Quadrilateral | 4 | 360° |
| Pentagon | 5 | 540° |
| Hexagon | 6 | 720° |
| Octagon | 8 | 1080° |

**Measure of one interior angle of a regular n-gon:**
> Each angle = (n − 2) · 180° / n

### Polygon Exterior Angles Theorem
The sum of the exterior angles of **any** convex polygon (one per vertex) is always **360°**.

> Each exterior angle of a regular n-gon = 360° / n

## 7.2 – Properties of Parallelograms

### Parallelogram
A quadrilateral with **both pairs of opposite sides parallel**.

### Properties of Parallelograms

| Property | Statement |
|---|---|
| Opposite sides | Opposite sides are congruent |
| Opposite angles | Opposite angles are congruent |
| Consecutive angles | Consecutive angles are supplementary (sum to 180°) |
| Diagonals | Diagonals **bisect** each other |

**Example:** In parallelogram ABCD, if ∠A = 70°, then ∠C = 70° and ∠B = ∠D = 110°.

---

## 7.3 – Proving a Quadrilateral is a Parallelogram

A quadrilateral is a parallelogram if **any one** of the following conditions holds:

1. Both pairs of opposite sides are **parallel**
2. Both pairs of opposite sides are **congruent**
3. Both pairs of opposite angles are **congruent**
4. One pair of opposite sides is **both parallel and congruent**
5. The diagonals **bisect each other**

## 7.4 – Properties of Special Parallelograms

### Rectangle
A parallelogram with **four right angles**.

#### Rectangle Diagonals Theorem
The diagonals of a rectangle are **congruent**.
> AC ≅ BD

### Rhombus
A parallelogram with **four congruent sides**.

#### Rhombus Diagonals Theorems
1. The diagonals of a rhombus are **perpendicular**.
2. Each diagonal of a rhombus **bisects** a pair of opposite angles.

### Square
A parallelogram that is **both a rectangle and a rhombus** (four right angles and four congruent sides).

### Concept Summary

| Shape | Right Angles | Equal Sides | Diagonals |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parallelogram | No | Opposite sides ≅ | Bisect each other |
| Rectangle | Yes (all 4) | Opposite sides ≅ | Congruent & bisect each other |
| Rhombus | No | All 4 ≅ | ⊥ bisectors of each other |
| Square | Yes (all 4) | All 4 ≅ | Congruent, ⊥, and bisect each other |

## 7.5 – Properties of Trapezoids and Kites

### Trapezoid
A quadrilateral with **exactly one pair of parallel sides**.
- **Bases:** the parallel sides
- **Legs:** the non-parallel sides
- **Base angles:** two angles that share a base

### Isosceles Trapezoid
A trapezoid with **congruent legs**.

#### Isosceles Trapezoid Theorems
1. Each pair of base angles is congruent.
2. The diagonals are congruent.

### Midsegment of a Trapezoid
The segment connecting the midpoints of the legs of a trapezoid.

#### Trapezoid Midsegment Theorem
The midsegment of a trapezoid is:
1. **Parallel** to both bases
2. **Half the sum** of the lengths of the bases

> Midsegment = ½ · (b₁ + b₂)

### Kite
A quadrilateral with **two pairs of consecutive congruent sides** (but opposite sides are not congruent).

#### Kite Theorems
1. The diagonals of a kite are **perpendicular**.
2. Exactly **one pair** of opposite angles is congruent (the angles between the non-congruent sides).

## Quick Reference – Quadrilateral Properties

| Shape | Opp. Sides ∥ | Opp. Sides ≅ | Opp. ∠s ≅ | Diagonals |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Parallelogram | Both pairs | Both pairs | Yes | Bisect each other |
| Rectangle | Both pairs | Both pairs | Yes | Congruent; bisect each other |
| Rhombus | Both pairs | All sides | Yes | ⊥; bisect angles |
| Square | Both pairs | All sides | Yes | Congruent; ⊥; bisect angles |
| Trapezoid | One pair only | Legs ≅ (isosceles) | Base ∠s ≅ (isosceles) | Congruent (isosceles) |
| Kite | None | Consecutive pairs | One pair | Perpendicular |
