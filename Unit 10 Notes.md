# Unit 10 – Circles

---

## 10.1 – Lines and Segments of Circles

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Circle (⊙P)** | The set of all points equidistant from a given point (the center) |
| **Radius** | A segment whose endpoints are the center and any point on the circle |
| **Diameter** | A segment whose endpoints lie on the circle and contains the center; d = 2r |
| **Chord** | A segment whose endpoints are both on the circle |
| **Secant** | A line that intersects a circle at exactly **two** points |
| **Tangent** | A line that intersects a circle at exactly **one** point (the point of tangency) |
| **Concentric Circles** | Coplanar circles that share the same center |

### Tangent Lines to Circles Theorem
In a plane, a line is **tangent** to a circle if and only if the line is **perpendicular to the radius** at the point of tangency.
> If PQ ⊥ l, then l is tangent to ⊙P

### External Tangent Congruence Theorem
Tangent segments from a **common external point** are congruent.
> If QS and RS are tangent to ⊙P from external point S, then QS ≅ RS

## 10.2 – Arcs and Central Angles

### Central Angle
An angle whose vertex is the **center** of the circle.

### Types of Arcs

| Arc Type | Measure | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor arc** | Less than 180° | Named with 2 letters (e.g., AB̂) |
| **Major arc** | Greater than 180° | Named with 3 letters (e.g., AĈB) |
| **Semicircle** | Exactly 180° | Arc whose endpoints are a diameter |

### Arc Measure
The measure of a **minor arc** equals the measure of its central angle.
> m arc = m central angle

**Example:** If central angle = 110°, then:
1. mRS̃ = 110°
2. mR̃TS = 360° − 110° = 250°
3. mR̃ST = 180°

### Adjacent Arcs
Two arcs on the same circle that intersect at exactly one point.

### Arc Addition Postulate
The measure of an arc formed by two adjacent arcs equals the **sum** of the two arcs.
> mÃB + mB̃C = mÃBC

## 10.3 – Chords

### Chords and Arcs Theorem
In the same circle (or congruent circles), two chords are congruent if and only if their corresponding arcs are congruent.
> AB ≅ CD ↔ mÃB = mĈD

### Perpendicular Chord Bisector Theorem
If a diameter is perpendicular to a chord, then it **bisects** the chord and its arc.

### Perpendicular Chord Bisector Converse
If one chord of a circle is a perpendicular bisector of another chord, then the first chord is a **diameter**.

**Example:** Given HN = NK = 7 and m∠HJK with JK ⊥ HK:
- HK = HN + NK = 7 + 7 = **14**
- 11x = 70 + x → 10x = 70 → x = 7

## 10.4 – Inscribed Angles

### Inscribed Angle
An angle whose **vertex lies on the circle** and whose sides are chords.

### Inscribed Angle Theorem
The measure of an inscribed angle is **half** the measure of its intercepted arc.
> m∠B = ½ · mÃC

**Example:** Find m∠T and mQ̃R when mR̃S = 48° and m∠R = 50°:
- m∠T = ½ · mR̃S = ½ · 48 = **24°**
- mT̃Q = 100°; mQ̃R = 180° − 100° = **80°**

### Inscribed Angles of a Circle Theorem
If two inscribed angles intercept the **same arc**, then the angles are **congruent**.

### Inscribed Quadrilateral Theorem
A quadrilateral can be inscribed in a circle if and only if its **opposite angles are supplementary**.

## 10.5 – Angles Inside and Outside Circles

### Angle Inside the Circle Theorem
If two chords intersect **inside** a circle, then the measure of each angle is one-half the **sum** of the intercepted arcs.
> m∠1 = m∠3 = ½(mÃC + mD̃B)
> m∠2 = m∠4 = ½(mÃD + mB̃C)

### Angle Outside the Circle Theorem
If a tangent and a secant, two tangents, or two secants intersect **outside** a circle, then the measure of the angle formed is one-half the **difference** of the intercepted arcs.
> m∠1 = ½(far arc − close arc) = ½(mD̃E − mB̃C)

---

## 10.6 – Segment Lengths in Circles

### Chord Segments Theorem
If two chords intersect inside a circle, then the **products of their segments** are equal.
> EA · EB = EC · ED

### Segments of Secants Theorem
If two secants share an endpoint outside a circle, then:
> (external segment) · (whole secant) = (external segment) · (whole secant)
> AB · AD = AC · AE

### Segments of Secants and Tangents Theorem
If a secant and a tangent segment share an endpoint outside a circle, then:
> (tangent segment)² = (external secant segment) · (whole secant)

## Quick Reference – Angle and Arc Relationships

| Location of Angle | Type | Formula |
|---|---|---|
| At center | Central angle | m∠ = m arc |
| On circle | Inscribed angle | m∠ = ½ · (intercepted arc) |
| Inside circle | Two chords cross | m∠ = ½(arc₁ + arc₂) |
| Outside circle | Secant/tangent | m∠ = ½(far arc − close arc) |
