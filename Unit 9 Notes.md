## 9.1 – Pythagorean Theorem

### Pythagorean Theorem
In a **right triangle**, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the two legs.
> a² + b² = c²   (where c is the hypotenuse)

### Pythagorean Triples
A set of three positive integers a, b, and c that satisfy a² + b² = c².

**Common triples:**
- 3, 4, 5 → 9 + 16 = 25 ✓
- 5, 12, 13
- 6, 8, 10
- 8, 15, 17

### Converse of the Pythagorean Theorem
If a² + b² = c², then the triangle is a right triangle.

| Relationship | Triangle Type |
|---|---|
| a² + b² = c² | Right triangle |
| a² + b² > c² | Acute triangle |
| a² + b² < c² | Obtuse triangle |

## 9.2 – Special Right Triangles

### 45°-45°-90° Triangle
A right isosceles triangle where both legs are equal.
- **Legs:** x
- **Hypotenuse:** x√2

> If leg = 5, then hypotenuse = 5√2

### 30°-60°-90° Triangle
- **Short leg (opposite 30°):** x
- **Long leg (opposite 60°):** x√3
- **Hypotenuse (opposite 90°):** 2x

> If short leg = 4, then long leg = 4√3 and hypotenuse = 8

## 9.3 – Similar Right Triangles

### Geometric Mean (Altitude) Theorem
When the altitude is drawn from the right angle to the hypotenuse of a right triangle, it creates two smaller triangles that are both similar to the original and to each other.

> altitude² = (left segment) · (right segment)

### Geometric Mean (Leg) Theorem
Each leg of the original right triangle is the geometric mean of the hypotenuse and the adjacent segment of the hypotenuse.

> leg² = hypotenuse · (adjacent segment)

## 9.4 – Tangent Ratio

### Tangent (tan)
For a right triangle with angle θ:
> tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent

**Example – Solve for x:**
- θ = 32°, opposite = 11, adjacent = x
- tan(32°) = 11/x → x = 11/tan(32°) → **x ≈ 17.6**

> ⚠️ Make sure your calculator is in **DEGREE MODE**

## 9.5 – Sine and Cosine Ratios

### Sine (sin)
> sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse

### Cosine (cos)
> cos(θ) = adjacent / hypotenuse

### SOH-CAH-TOA Memory Device

| Ratio | Acronym | Formula |
|---|---|---|
| **sin** | **S**OH | **S**ine = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse |
| **cos** | CA**H** | **C**osine = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse |
| **tan** | TO**A** | **T**angent = **O**pposite / **A**djacent |

**Example – Find sin(A) and cos(A):**
- Given a right triangle with legs 3 and 2, hypotenuse = √13
- sin(A) = 2/√13, cos(A) = 3/√13

## 9.6 – Inverse Trig Functions and Solving Right Triangles

### Inverse Trig Functions
Used to find an **angle** when you know two sides.

| Function | Formula | Also Written As |
|---|---|---|
| Sine Inverse | θ = sin⁻¹(opp/hyp) | θ = arcsin(opp/hyp) |
| Cosine Inverse | θ = cos⁻¹(adj/hyp) | θ = arccos(adj/hyp) |
| Tangent Inverse | θ = tan⁻¹(opp/adj) | θ = arctan(opp/adj) |

> ⚠️ Note: sin⁻¹(x) ≠ (sin(x))⁻¹

### Solving a Right Triangle
To fully **solve** a right triangle means finding all unknown side lengths and angle measures.

**Example:** Given AB = 3, BC = 2, m∠B = 90°, find AC, m∠A, and m∠C:
- AC = √(3² + 2²) = √13 ≈ 3.61
- m∠A = tan⁻¹(2/3) ≈ **33.69°**
- m∠C = 90° − 33.69° = **56.31°**
