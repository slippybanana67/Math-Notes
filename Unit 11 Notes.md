# Unit 11 – Circumference, Area, and Volume

---

## 11.1 – Circumference and Arc Length

### Circumference
The distance around a circle.
> C = πd = 2πr

**Example:** Circle with r = 9 → C = 2π(9) = **18π ≈ 56.55**

### Arc Length
The portion of a circle's circumference corresponding to a given arc.
> Arc length of AB = (mAB / 360°) · 2πr

**Example:** Find arc length of AB with r = 8 and central angle = 60°:
> Arc length = (60°/360°) · 2π(8) = (1/6) · 16π = **8π/3 ≈ 8.38**

### Radians
Another unit for measuring angles.

**Degrees to Radians:**
> φ = (π / 180°) · θ

**Radians to Degrees:**
> θ = (180° / π) · φ

**Examples:**
- Convert 45° to radians: φ = (π/180°) · 45° = **π/4**
- Convert π/6 radians to degrees: θ = (180°/π) · (π/6) = **30°**

## 11.2 – Area of Circles and Sectors

### Area of a Circle
> A = πr²

### Sector
The region of a circle bounded by **two radii** and their intercepted arc (a "pie slice").

### Area of a Sector
> Area of sector = (mAB / 360°) · πr²

**Example:** Sector with r = 6 and central angle = 90°:
> Area = (90°/360°) · π(6²) = (1/4) · 36π = **9π ≈ 28.27**

---

## 11.3 – Area of Regular Polygons

### Area of Rhombuses and Kites
> A = ½ · d₁ · d₂   (where d₁ and d₂ are the diagonals)

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Apothem (a)** | The perpendicular distance from the center of a regular polygon to one of its sides |
| **Central angle** | The angle formed at the center between two consecutive radii to vertices |

### Central Angle of a Regular Polygon
> Central angle = 360° / n   (where n = number of sides)

### Area of a Regular Polygon
> A = ½ · a · P   (where a = apothem, P = perimeter)

**Examples:**
- Equilateral triangle, side = 8, apothem = 4√3/3: A = ½ · (4√3/3) · 24 = **16√3**
- Regular hexagon, apothem = 5√3, side = 10: A = ½ · 5√3 · 60 = **150√3**

---

## 11.4 – Volumes of Prisms and Cylinders

### Prism
A solid with two congruent, parallel polygonal bases connected by rectangular faces.

### Volume of a Prism
> V = Bh   (where B = area of base, h = height)

**Example:** Rectangular prism with base 6 × 11 and height 14:
> B = 6 · 11 = 66; V = 66 · 14 = **924**

### Volume of a Cylinder
> V = Bh = πr²h

**Example:** Cylinder with r = 8 km and h = 7 km:
> V = π(8²)(7) = 448π ≈ **1407.4 km³**

---

## 11.5 – Volumes of Pyramids

### Pyramid
A solid with one polygonal base and triangular faces that meet at a single point (apex).

### Volume of a Pyramid
> V = ⅓ · B · h   (where B = area of base, h = height)

The volume of a pyramid is **one-third** the volume of a prism with the same base and height.

**Example:** Square pyramid with base side 2 in. and height 3 in.:
> B = 4 in²; V = ⅓ · 4 · 3 = **4 in³**

---

## 11.6 – Surface Area and Volume of Cones

### Right Cone
A solid with a circular base and a lateral surface that tapers to a vertex (apex).

**Key dimensions:**
- r = radius of base
- l = slant height (distance from apex to edge of base along lateral surface)
- h = height (perpendicular distance from base to apex)

### Surface Area of a Right Cone
> SA = πr² + πrl   (base area + lateral area)

**Example:** Cone with r = 4 and l = 6:
> SA = π(4²) + π(4)(6) = 16π + 24π = **40π ≈ 125.7**

### Volume of a Cone
> V = ⅓ · πr²h

---

## 11.7 – Surface Area and Volume of Spheres

### Sphere
The set of all points in space equidistant from a given center point.

### Surface Area of a Sphere
> SA = 4πr²

### Volume of a Sphere
> V = (4/3) · πr³

**Example:** Soccer ball with r = 4.5 in.:
> SA = 4π(4.5²) = 81π ≈ 254.5 in²
> V = (4/3)π(4.5³) ≈ **381.7 in³**
